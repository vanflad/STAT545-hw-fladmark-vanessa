Powers

The goal of powers is to calculate the exponential power of a number! There are a variety of functions to choose from, such as square(), cube(), reciprocal() and pow() for calculating to the power of two, the power of three, the inverse of a power 1/Xa and the power of any given number a, respectively.

Example

Here are some basic examples which shows you how to solve common power calculations:

pow(-2, 4)
## [1] 16
square(1:10)
##  [1]   1   4   9  16  25  36  49  64  81 100
cube(-5)
## [1] -125
reciprocal(-2, 3)
## [1] -0.125

Figures

The figures show numbers 1 through 10 and what some common exponential power transformations of that vector look like when displayed visually.

plot(1:10, square(1:10))
plot(1:10, cube(1:10))
plot(1:10, pow(1:10, 4))
plot(1:10, reciprocal(1:10, 2))
